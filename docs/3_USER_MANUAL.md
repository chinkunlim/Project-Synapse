## 1. The Global Console
At the bottom of every page sits the **Global Console**. This is your command center's feedback log.
*   **Activity Feed**: Watch this area for status updates like "Syncing...", "Success", or "Error".
*   **Resize**: Drag the top border to resize the console.
*   **Minimize**: Double-click the header maximize/minimize to save screen space.
*   **Auto-Hide**: The page layout automatically adjusts when you toggle the console.

*   **To-Do List**: Aggregates tasks from your notion "Action Items" database. Check the boxes to mark them done in Notion instantly.
*   **NDHU Tasks**: Syncs with your Google Tasks to show university-related administrative work.

## 3. üè´ Google Classroom Manager
Manage your courses and students without leaving the app.

### **Connecting to Classroom**
1.  Click the **"Connect Classroom"** button.
2.  Authorize with your Google Account.
3.  Select your role (Teacher/Student/All) and click **"Load Courses"**.

### **Managing a Course**
Click on any course card to reveal management tools:
*   **Student List**: View roster and export to Excel.
*   **Assignments**: Create new assignments, set due dates, and assign to specific students.
*   **Materials**: Publish lecture notes or links.
*   **Topic Creator**: Batch create weekly topics (e.g., "Week 1", "Week 2"...) in seconds.

---

## 4. üìÑ Thesis Compilation Factory
A powerful tool for academic writing. Convert Markdown into professionally formatted PDFs.

### **How to Compile a Thesis**
1.  **Format Selection**: Choose between "APA 7th" or "CJP" (Chinese Journal of Psychology).
2.  **Metadata**: Fill in the Title, Author, and Abstract (Chinese & English).
3.  **Upload Files**:
    *   **Markdown (.md)**: Your main content file.
    *   **Bibliography (.bib)**: Your references. Ensure citation keys match (e.g., `@smith2020`).
    *   **Figures**: Upload any images referenced in your text.
4.  **Compile**: Click **"Start Compilation"**. The system will process the LaTeX and download the PDF automatically.
